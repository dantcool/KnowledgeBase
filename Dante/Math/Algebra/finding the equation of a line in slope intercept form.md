
$y=Mx+B$

m is the slope 
b is the y int where the line touches the y axis 

find the slope then plug into point slope form equation 

$y-y1 = m(x-x1)$

example 

Find the equation of the line (in slope intercept form) passing through the points (4,1) and (-1,5)

find the slope using $\frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}$

we get $\frac{5-1}{-1-4}$ = $\frac{4}{-5}$ this is our slope or M

then plug into point slope form equation giving us 

$y-1 = \frac{-4}{5}(x-4)$
= $y-1 = -\frac{4}{5} + \frac{21}{5}$
=$y = -\frac{4}{5}x + \frac{21}{5}$
and this is our answer
